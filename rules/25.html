I-Resquer

“I walk slowly, never walk backwards” –Abraham Lincon

PROBLEM STATEMENT: The objective of this contest is for a manually controlled bot to place the box in their respective coloured places. The robot cannot traverse back, or step on the path with the colour of the box it is holding.

Team specification:

1. Maximum 4 members per team.
2. Students from different colleges, sections, stream and years can form a team.
3. Only one bot per team will be allowed and no two team will be allowed to share their bots.
4. No person shall be associated with two teams.
5. Use of Cell phone or Camera by the Participants at the arena is not allowed.

ROBOT SPECIFICATIONS:

• The robot must be completely manual. It should not receive any help from computers during the course of its run.
• The robots should have the capability to traverse different terrains, and lift up cubes (side 10 to 15 cm).
• The robot has to be developed and designed by the team itself. The usage of commercially available sensors, development boards is not allowed, it is forbidden to use a complete robotic system which is sold ready for the purpose of the competition (like LEGO kits).
• The size of the robot must not exceed 25cm * 25cm * 25cm. Individual parameters to be measured.
• Robots or participants must not damage the tournament area.
• The robot may use on board battery, or using adapters of 9-12V, max 1A.
• In case of external power supplies, the wire must slag all the time, pulling wires to alter the movement of the robots WILL NOT BE TOLERATED at all and the responsible team will be DISQUALIFED. The extension cords have to be arranged by the participants.

Competition Rules

1. The time of any run is measured from the robot starts moving from the red square to the home.
2. For each successful box placement the robot will be awarded 100 points.
3. The robot is not allowed to touch the “no touch zones” in the arena. Doing so will invite a penalty of 50 points.
4. The robot must go only in the forward direction. Going backwards will invite a penalty of 50 points.
5. A team may take a maximum of 1 RESTART. The robot will start again from the “START” point and the timer will be RESET. It will be considered as a NEW RUN. The teams can check the calibration and other physical parts of the robot at this time. But programming the robot is NOT ALLOWED. But teams taking a RESTART will incur a penalty of 100 points.
6. A team can take a maximum of 2 TIMEOUTS of 2 min each. The robot will start from the last checkpoint in case it takes a TIMEOUT. During this time the physical connections and mechanical parts can be repaired, but addition or alteration of new techniques is prohibited.
7. Each team will be provided with one trial and one final run. Contact:
